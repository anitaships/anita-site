## Context

I work at a small, fast-moving cross-border AI SaaS team. As a growth & product operator — not an engineer — I kept hitting the same wall: repetitive operations work (pulling data, drafting outreach, formatting reports) ate the hours I needed for actual strategy.

## What I did

Instead of waiting for engineering time, I learned to build my own tools with Claude Code. Over a few months I shipped **43 internal "AI Skills" across 7 categories** — covering data pulls, content drafting, outreach scheduling, and reporting.

## How

- Treated every recurring manual task as a candidate for automation.
- Built, tested, and version-controlled each tool in git.
- Documented them so the rest of the team could reuse them.

## Outcome

- **43 AI-Skill tools across 7 categories**, in daily use.
- **2nd-highest git contribution on the team** — behind only the CTO, as a non-engineer.
- Repetitive operations turned into one-command workflows, freeing time for growth strategy.

## Why it matters

AI-native isn't a line on my resume — it's how I actually work. I'd rather build the tool than do the task twice.
